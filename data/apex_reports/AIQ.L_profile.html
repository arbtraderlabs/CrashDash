Enquiries AIQ Limited c/o +44 (0)20 7618 9100 Graham Duncan, Chairman Luther Pendragon (Media Relations) Claire Norbury +44 (0)20 7618 9100 Operational Review During the first half of the 2022 financial year, the Group focused on the delivery of a contract, secured at the end of the previous year, to supply a DeFi DEX to a customer based in Australia. For the project, the Group performs the role of project manager and subcontracts the technical delivery (such that the net benefit to the Group is the margin earned on the contract). The majority of the project was delivered during the first half, with completion occurring since period end. Also during the period the Group was awarded a contract to supply an NFT platform designed to enable art schools and education centres in Hong Kong to assist their students in publishing NFTs and developing their creative talent under Web3 technology. The Group will oversee and manage the project, with the development of the marketplace being provided by Accubits Technologies Inc., a full-service software provider, and digiXnode Technology Ltd., which specialises in blockchain development. Work on the platform is underway and the beta version is expected to be ready for testing in the coming months. Financial Review Revenue for the six months to 30 April 2022 was ¬£361k compared with ¬£12k for the first half of the previous year. The revenue was predominantly based on the delivery of the DeFi DEX contract (¬£331k), with ¬£10k from the NFT contract and a ¬£19k contribution from IT projects in Hong Kong. The Group recognised a gross profit of ¬£115k compared with a gross loss of ¬£197k for the first six months of the previous year. This was as a result of the higher revenue. Administrative expenses were reduced to ¬£392k (H1 2021: ¬£573k) reflecting a reduction in personnel costs of ¬£43k, Directors' fees of ¬£28k, and consultancy and other overhead savings of ¬£110k. In Malaysia, the Group was able to sublet around half of its office space during the period, which has been substantially increased post period, thereby serving to further reduce the Group's outgoings. The Group recognised a net gain on foreign exchange of ¬£70k compared with a net loss of ¬£138k for the same period of the prior year. The lower expenses combined with the higher revenue enabled a significant reduction in operating loss for the period to ¬£207k (H1 2021: ¬£908k loss). Net finance costs were ¬£5k compared with ¬£7k for the first half of the previous year. As a result, loss before tax for the period was reduced to ¬£202k (H1 2021: ¬£915k loss) and the loss per share to 0.3 pence (H1 2021: 1.4 pence loss per share). During the period, as announced on 25 January 2022, the Group raised ¬£500k from the issue of convertible loan notes. At 30 April 2022, the Group had cash and cash equivalents of ¬£852k (31 October 2021: ¬£582k). Outlook In the second half of the year, the Group has continued to deliver its IT consultancy projects and maintain tight control over costs. The Group expects revenue for the full year to be significantly higher than for the year to 31 October 2021 and anticipates a substantial reduction in net loss. However, the Group expects revenue for the second half of the year to be lower than that generated in the first half as the majority of the DeFi DEX contract was delivered during the first six-month period. The Board continues to closely monitor the progress of the Group and evaluate opportunities for generating value for shareholders. 6 May 2025 07:00 RNS Number : 2989H AIQ Limited 06 May 2025 6 May 2025 AIQ Limited ("AIQ" or the "Company") AIQ establishes strategic partnership with Centslink MoU signed to pursue data centre construction projects AIQ (LSE: AIQ) announces that it has signed a Memorandum of Understanding ("MoU") with Centslink, a technology-driven enterprise specialising in the design, construction and delivery of data centre infrastructure and management, for the purpose of pursuing data centre construction projects in Southeast Asia. Headquartered in Dubai, Centslink provides comprehensive solutions tailored to the needs of clients across the Middle East, Africa, Asia-Pacific, and other key global markets. It offers end-to-end data centre development services, covering every stage from site selection and feasibility studies through to architectural design, systems integration, project execution, management commissioning, operations and maintenance. With a highly experienced engineering team and a strong track record, Centslink has delivered modular and scalable data centre projects for sectors including energy, government, finance, and telecommunications. The demand for data centres in Southeast Asia is growing rapidly, with the value of the data centre construction market forecast to increase from $5.4bn in 2024 to $11.8bn in 2030 ( source ). AIQ and Centslink intend to seek to capitalise on this development by jointly pursuing data centre projects, leveraging the experience and expertise of Centslink and the local knowledge and network of AIQ as well as its status as a publicly-listed company on the London Stock Exchange. As part of this, Centslink also intends to introduce AIQ to its partners, which represents further potential opportunities for the Company. However, there can be no guarantee that any of this activity will result in the award of a contract or the generation of revenue. Li Chun Chung, Executive Director of AIQ, said: "We are delighted to have entered this strategic partnership with Centslink. The market for data centres in Southeast Asia is significant and by combining our expertise and experience, we are well-positioned to capitalise on the demand for the construction of new facilities. We hope to update shareholders on our progress in due course."
