Holdings PLC 03 November 2025 REACH: non-regulatory announcement* Light Science Technologies Holdings plc (" LSTH ", " Light Science ", the " Company " or the " Group ") UK All Party Parliamentary Group Calls for Action on Food Production Technology, innovation & smarter solutions required to ensure UK food security Light Science Technologies Holdings plc (AIM: LST), the innovative technology and manufacturing business providing real-world solutions targeting issues including global food security and fire safety, notes the release, and welcomes the recommendations, of the All Party Parliamentary Group, Science & Technology in Agriculture ("APPGSTA") report titled "Feeding Britain Sustainably to 2050: the 30:50:50 mission". The APPGSTA encourages the UK Government to adopt a target of 30% greater agricultural output while halving the sector's environmental footprint by 2050. In doing so, it is recommended to develop a National Farm Data initiative, incentivise real-time data capture and AI-enabled decision-making tools, and perform a root-and-branch R&D review with a view to producing more food from less land, using less water and energy. The report makes it clear that the US, EU, and OECD-FAO have already committed to driving innovation-led productivity growth and that the UK risks falling behind unless it embraces data-driven, technology-enabled agriculture, and creates strong policy momentum for business. In making its case, the APPGSTA notes that farming productivity growth has stalled due to fragmented support policies, over-precautionary regulation, and underuse of science. The result is a gap that must be closed between discovery science and farming practice. LSTH's AgTech division offers a suite of tailored agricultural solutions that encompass real time data control systems, grow lights, sensor technology, venting, and irrigation, alongside AI technology, directly enabling progress towards the report's headline objectives. Supporting applications in indoor, vertical, glasshouse, hybrid polytunnel, and broadacre farming, its products are designed to increase crop yield and health with less input and smarter intervention. Simon Deacon, CEO of LSTH, commented: " We are extremely pleased to see that a number of the recommendations in today's report align with the messages that we have been championing. It has been clear to many in the industry for some time that we need a smarter, modern approach to the way we farm if we are to ensure we have the food we need, and it is absolutely right that the UK treats food security as a statutory national priority. "We are proud of the pioneering work we have already been doing in this sector and believe that we have the solutions to provide customers with the tools to combat growing threats to food production both in the UK and globally. Regulatory pressure will continue to grow around the world and LSTH is well positioned to help growers meet emerging challenges and support Government in driving effective change."
